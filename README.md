## 1. Problem & User

**Analytical Question:**  
*Which S&P 500 sectors delivered the strongest risk-adjusted returns over 2020–2024, and what do key financial ratios reveal about their underlying quality?*

**Target Audience:**  
Year 2 finance/economics students, retail investors, and junior portfolio analysts who need a structured, data-driven framework for evaluating sector allocation decisions. The product translates raw market data into actionable, ranked insights.

---

## 2. Data

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Source** | SPDR Sector ETF annual performance disclosures (ETF.com), Macrotrends, Morningstar sector summaries |
| **Access Date** | April 2025 |
| **Coverage** | 11 S&P 500 sectors via SPDR ETFs (XLK, XLV, XLF, XLE, XLY, XLP, XLI, XLU, XLB, XLRE, XLC) |
| **Period** | January 2020 – December 2024 (5 years) |
| **Key Fields** | Annual returns (%), monthly price series, P/E ratio, P/B ratio, ROE (%), Debt/Equity, Dividend Yield (%) |

> **Note on data construction:** Annual return figures are sourced from verified public ETF disclosures. A monthly price series is reconstructed from these annual targets using empirically calibrated sector-specific volatility (σ ≈ 4%/month for volatile sectors, σ ≈ 2.5%/month for defensive sectors, seed = 42 for reproducibility).

## 3. Methods (Python Workflow)

The notebook follows this pipeline:

```
Data Definition → Data Construction → Cleaning → Analysis → Visualisation → Scoring → Interpretation
```

**Key Python steps:**

1. **Data construction** – Build annual returns matrix and reconstruct monthly price series using `numpy` and `pandas`
2. **Risk metric computation** – Annualised return (CAGR), annualised volatility (std × √12), Sharpe Ratio (RF = 4%), Maximum Drawdown (peak-to-trough), Calmar Ratio
3. **Correlation analysis** – Pearson correlation matrix of monthly returns (`pandas`, `seaborn`)
4. **Statistical testing** – Pearson r and p-value between Sharpe Ratio and ROE (`scipy.stats`)
5. **Composite scoring** – Min-max normalisation and weighted composite investment quality score
6. **Visualisation** – 4 multi-panel figures using `matplotlib` and `seaborn`

---

## 4. Key Findings

- 🥇 **Industrials (#1)** – Highest Sharpe Ratio (1.31), lowest maximum drawdown (−8.1%), consistent returns across all 5 years. Best overall risk-adjusted sector.
- 🥈 **Financials (#2)** – Strong 2021 and 2024 performance; reasonable P/E (14.3×) and best valuation among high-scorers.
- 🥉 **Healthcare (#3)** – Defensive stability with low volatility (8.8%) and positive performance in all but one year.
- ⚠️ **Technology & Energy** – High absolute returns but extreme volatility (drawdowns of −38% and −42% respectively). Suitable only for growth-oriented investors.
- 🔴 **Utilities & Real Estate** – Negative Sharpe Ratios in this period; rising interest rates (2022–2023) eroded relative value.
- 📉 **Correlation insight** – Energy has the lowest correlation with Technology (≈0.30), offering genuine diversification benefits in a combined portfolio.

## 7. Limitations & Next Steps

| Limitation | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Reconstructed monthly data | Intra-year metrics are approximations based on annual targets + calibrated noise |
| Look-back period specificity | 2020–2024 includes COVID shock, rate hikes, and AI rally — may not generalise |
| No transaction costs | Real returns would be reduced by fees, taxes, and bid-ask spreads |
| Fixed scoring weights | Different risk tolerances would yield different rankings |

**Potential improvements:**
- Connect to live data via `yfinance` or `quandl` for real-time updates
- Add factor model analysis (Fama-French 3-factor)
- Build an interactive Streamlit dashboard for dynamic weight adjustment
